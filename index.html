NORTH

CUBA

In the Caribbean region, south of Florida, lies the island of Cuba. One of the most striking observations made by visitors are the amount of vintage cars filling the roads throughout this island country. In 1962, the United States imposed an embargo which prevented the US market from conducting trade and businesses with Cuba. This especially made imports of new vehicles extremely difficult, and it forced Cubans to preserve and maintain their already existing cars for a long time to come.
Cuban culture
Cuban culture Another concept often connected to this country is music and dance. These elements are deeply integrated in the Cuban culture and plays a vital role in gatherings and events as well as the expression of the island’s identity and spirit. Some well-known dance styles that originate from Cuba are salsa, rumba and cha-cha-cha, each pulsating with rhythms and charismatic movement drawing bystanders to the dance floor. And in the midst of such vibrant atmosphere, one can’t do any other than ache for a sip of a cool and refreshing mojito, right? This iconic Cuban cocktail, first created in the capital Havana in the 16th century, is a mix of white rum, sugar, soda water, lime juice and fresh mint leaves to help cool down on a hot day and increase relaxation.

MEXICO

¡Bienvenido a México! A land of enchantment where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modern life, and every corner invites you to explore, indulge, and celebrate the beauty of this remarkable country. 
Chichen Itza
Nestled in the heart of the Yucatan Peninsula lies Chichen Itza, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
This ancient Mayan city, with its towering pyramids and intricate stone carvings, offers a glimpse into Mexico’s pre-Columbian past.
Tequila
Born from the blue agave plant, is Mexico's liquid treasure. Crafted with care, it's a fiery elixir that ignites the senses and fosters bonds between people. Each sip is a taste of tradition, a celebration of life, and a vibrant reminder of Mexico's spirit.
Día de Muertos
Another great part of Mexico’s culture is their most cherished tradition Día de Muertos, or Day of the Dead. It is a colorful and joyful celebration honoring deceased loved ones. It’s a time when the boundary between the living and the dead blurs, as Mexicans embrace death as a natural part of life. 

UNITED STATES

As one of the largest countries in the world, the United States of America represents a rich blend of culture, urban environments, and innovation, making it one of the most influential forces across the world. This country can proudly claim the title of being the birthplace of one of the largest brands globally, Coca-Cola, which was established in Atlanta, Georgia in 1982. It also possesses the status of serving as the most substantial hub for the fast-food market through offering everything from beloved and worldwide, classic food-chains to regional and local specialties.
New York City
Besides being influential within the food and beverages market, the US is also often associated with cultural vibrancy, thanks to its 50 states, each with its own unique identity, heritage, and sceneries. New York City is the largest city in the US by population and is a famous global center that attracts millions of tourists every year. With world-class museums, iconic landmarks such as the Statue of Liberty and the bustling Times Square in the center of the action, it's not hard to see why this city keeps attracting attention. In addition to this, New York City is also celebrated for its lively street art scenes where colorful murals buildings meet complex graffiti tags in hidden alleyways, transforming ordinary street art.

Its unique geography, stretching over 4,300 kilometers from north to south, provides a variety of climates ideal for cultivating a wide range of grape varieties. This diversity, combined with a rich winemaking heritage and modern innovation, has solidified Chile's reputation as a top wine producer.

ENGLAND

England, part of the United Kingdom, is a country steeped in history and tradition, yet it remains at the forefront of innovation and culture. Located in the southern part of the island of Great Britain, England has played a significant role in shaping the modern world, from the Industrial Revolution to the British Empire. Here's an overview of England, with a focus on London, including key facts about the country and the city. With a population of over 56 million people, England is the most populous country in the UK.
London
London England's capital, London, is one of the world's great cities, known for its iconic landmarks, cultural institutions, and diverse population. The Houses of Parliament, along with the Elizabeth Tower (commonly known as Big Ben), are iconic symbols of British governance and democracy. Westminster Abbey, a gothic church where British monarchs are crowned and buried, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a significant historical site.
Big Ben is one of the most recognizable landmarks in London and a symbol of the city itself. Often mistakenly used to refer to the entire clock tower, Big Ben actually denotes the Great Bell within the tower, while the structure as a whole is known as the Elizabeth Tower. The bell's iconic chimes have become synonymous with London and are broadcast globally, particularly during New Year's Eve celebrations and major national events.

FRANCE

France, a country often associated with love and romance in the eyes of many. As its capital, the romantic atmosphere that is Paris, lets you experience pompous architecture and elegance while strolling down the alluring streets, passing charming boutiques and cafés on your way to buy your morning croissant. In the heart of the city stands the grandiose Eiffel Tower, built in 1887 and named after the designer Gustave Eiffel, and if you’relucky enough to visit in February/March or September/October, you might also catch a glimpse of the prestigious event, Paris Fashion Week, attracting celebrities worldwide.
Vineyards
If relocating outside of the city core, you will embark on a journey through the scenic and picturesque countryside of France, where the quaint villages and famous vineyards are located. The Champagne region covers part of the northeast and is the place where the prestigious sparkling wine, Champagne, is crafted.

GERMANY

Germany is a country with cultural traditions, and old landmarks. Its landscape is dotted with majestic castles. It is believed that there are around 25,000 castles and castle ruins throughout Germany. These range from small fortifications and medieval strongholds to grand fairytale-palaces like Neuschwanstein Castle in Bavaria and Burg Eltz in the Rhineland.
Oktoberfest
One of the most famous festivals is Oktoberfest, the world’s largest Volksfest held annually in Munich. This iconic event attracts
millions of visitors from around the world who gather to indulge in traditional Bavarian beer, hearty cuisine, and lively music in massive beer tents adorned with colorful decorations in blue and white.  
Porsche
A legendary automotive marque, founded in 1931 by Ferdinand Porsche, the brand has become synonymous with precision engineering, performance, and timeless design. Nestled in the town of Stuttgart, Porsche's heritage is deeply intertwined with German automotive culture.

GREECE

Greece is the place where the Acropolis of Athens stands tall, like a high city on a rocky hill. It's an ancient place full of amazing old buildings and art. These old buildings are the best examples of Greek history and culture. The most famous one is called The Parthenon, and it's super cool!
Santorini
Heading away from Athens, you'll find Santorini, a group of islands in the sunny Cyclades. Here at Santorini you can find one of the few still active volcanos in Greece and Europe. Long ago, this volcano made Santorini what it is today. But Santorini isn't just about the volcano. It's also famous for its beautiful white buildings with blue roofs. They're everywhere you look, and they're so pretty!
The Olympics
Did you know that Greece had the first ever Olympic Games? Yep, way back in 776 BC, people from all over Greece gathered to run, jump, wrestle, and more. It was like a big sports party that lasted for five days! Everyone came together for fun and games, just like a huge barbecue with friends.

ICELAND

Velkomin til íslands! Situated on the edge of the Artic Circle, Iceland is a land of extremes, where fire and ice converge to create a landscape truly unlike any other. 
Volcanos and Geysers
One of Iceland’s most striking features is its terrain with majestic volcanos and dramatic lava fields. Here you can find over 30 active volcanic systems, including the infamous Eyjafjallajökull. Because of the volcanos, the beaches become black. Adding to Iceland’s geological wonders are its geysers, natural hot springs that erupt with scalding water and steam.
Whales and sharks
Iceland’s coastal waters are teeming with life, making it one of the best places in the world for whale watching. A trip to Iceland would not be complete without sampling some of its unique culinary offerings, including Hákarl, fermented shark. Made since the 9th century and considered a delicacy by locals, However, it is worth noting that the shark is poisonous if not fermented properly, making it an acquired taste for adventurous foodies willing to brave its unique flavor.

ITALY

Italy, a country famed for its rich history, stunning landscapes, and cultural treasures that captivates the hearts of millions of visitors each year.  
Worlds most famous cuisine?
One cannot speak of Italy without mentioning its culinary heritage, which is as diverse as it is delicious. From the savory simplicity of a classic pizza to the creaminess of gelato or a pasta carbonara – every bite tells a story of tradition,
and the uncompromising pursuit of culinary perfection.
Colosseum
Among Italy’s architectural marvel stands wonders like the Leaning Tower of Pisa, Fontana di Trevi and not least the Colosseum, an ancient amphitheater steeped in history and grandeur. This iconic monument, once the epicenter of Roman entertainment, continues to inspire with its imposing facade and rich cultural significance.
Walking through its ancient passages, one can almost hear the echoes of gladiatorial combat and the cheers of spectators, reminding us of Italy’s enduring legacy as a cradle of civilization. 
Ferrari
The sleek curves of Ferrari sports cars, symbols of Italian excellence in engineering and design, roar through the picturesque countryside, embodying a passion for speed and precision that resonates deeply with the Italian spirit.

SWEDEN

Sweden, in Northern Europe, boasts captivating landscapes, progressive values, and a rich cultural heritage. With its commitment to gender equality, innovation, and sustainability, Sweden has become a global leader in various fields. From the midnight sun to the tradition of "fika," its charm attracts visitors worldwide, while its vibrant cities and welcoming atmosphere make it a beloved home for its residents.
ABBA
Abba is one of Sweden’s most famous music groups, the group was active between 1972 and 1982 and are recognized all over the world. The group name is an acronym of the first letters of the members first name; Agnetha, Benny, Björn and Anni-Frid. In 1974 Abba participated won the Eurovision song contest 1974, with their song Waterloo. Abba were Sweden´s first winner of the competition and this is where the group had its international breakthroughs.   
Swedish Fika
Something you can´t miss when visiting Sweden is the Swedish Fika, which has been famous for Sweden throughout the years. Fika is not a specific object, it´s more of a concept, a state of mind, an attitude, and an important part of Swedish culture. Fika doesn´t have a translation, but in English it could mean like “a coffee break”. A favorite sweet treat at the fika-table are cinnamon buns together with coffee, tea or another delicious drink.

AFRICA

EGYPT

In Egypt you can find a lot of pyramids, which are ancient masonry structures. It is at Giza, on the outskirts of Cairo, the most famous pyramids are located and found. The pyramids were built a long time ago, and during this time Egypt was one of the richest and most powerful civilizations in the world. Pyramids are some of the most magnificent man-made structures in history. 
Cleopatra
One of the most famous persons from Egypt is Cleopatra, who was an Egyptian queen during 51 to 30 BC. Cleopatra is famous for actively influenced Roman politics at a crucial period, but she is especially known for her relationships with Julius Caesar and Mark Antony. She came to represent the prototype of the romantic femme fatale, no other woman of antiquity did this. Nowadays a lot of numerous books, plays and movies are inspired by Cleopatra. 
The Nile River
The longest river in the world, holds profound significance to Egypt as it has been the lifeblood of the civilization for millennia, providing fertile land for agriculture, facilitating trade and transportation, and shaping the culture, economy, and history of the region through its annual flooding cycles, which sustained ancient Egyptian civilization and continue to be vital to the nation's prosperity and identity.

SOUTH AFRICA

Welkom in Suid Afrika! A land of breathtaking landscapes and vibrant diversity. From the rugged beauty of its wildlife reserves to the rolling vineyards of its wine regions, South Africa offers a tapestry of experiences waiting to be explored. Safari enthusiasts flock to South Africa’s renowned game reserves, such as Kruger National Park, to witness the iconic “Big Five” and a wealth of other wildlife in their natural habitat.
Rich wine regions
Venture into the picturesque wine regions of Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, and Pearl, nestled in the Western Cape’s fertile valleys. Indulge in wine tasting tours amidst lush vineyards, sampling world-class wines that reflect South Africa’s unique terroir and winemaking heritage.

ASIA

CHINA

China, in East Asia, boasts a millennia-old civilization and a booming economy led by the Communist Party. Its mix of ancient wonders and modern marvels, along with its vast population, make it a global powerhouse shaping trends in technology, trade, and culture.
The Great Wall of China
In the north of China, you can find the Great Wall of China, a series of military defense walls. It is the world’s longest construction, the world’s biggest military structure and one of the worlds most visited tourist attraction. The wall got listed as a world heritage by UNESCO 1987 and 2007 it got chosen as one of the New Seven Wonders. The parts of the wall that exists today have a total length of approximately 6000km.   
The Forbidden City
One of Chinas most famous tourist attraction is the Forbidden City, which is a palace complex you can find at the center of Imperial City of Beijing. The forbidden city served as the imperial palace for over five centuries and it remains one of the most significant cultural and historical landmarks, renowned for its majestic architecture, intricate design, and preservation of Chinese heritage.

INDIA

India, also known as the spice capital, proudly carries the title of being the largest producer of spices globally. Through this wide supply of spices, the Indian cuisine offers a broad range of rich and vibrant flavors serving everything from fiery chili peppers to aromatic cardamom and cumin, all playing a fundamental role to its culinary heritage and history.
Bollywood
Just as symbolic as the great cuisine, India is likewise famed for its successful and influential film industry,
which has its homebase in Bombay, now a days more known as Mumbai. As one of the largest and most outstanding film industries worldwide, it has developed its own unique identity and style throughout the years, focusing on Indian culture and heritage. There’s also an adequate amount of emphasis on dance and music, integrating sequences of song and dance movements as part of the storytelling to express emotions and entertain the audience, capturing both the Indian and global market.
Taj Mahal
And when one would think this could not be topped of, we simply cannot forget the fact that this country is also the home of the majestic Taj Mahal – an architectural masterpiece established in 1643, with blending elements of both Islamic, Persian and Indian styles. It’s been selected as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and has also been nominated as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, attracting over 5 million visitor per year from all around the world.

INDONESIA

Indonesia is an expansive archipelago consisting of over 17,000 islands, providing a diverse range of ecosystems that support a remarkable variety of wildlife. From dense rainforests to coastal mangroves, Indonesia is one of the world's most biodiverse countries. It is home to an array of unique species, many of which are endemic, meaning they are found nowhere else on earth. Monkeys are a significant part of Indonesia's wildlife. The long-tailed macaque is common in many areas, including tourist sites like Bali. Other species include the Javan langur and the Thomas's leaf monkey, each with unique characteristics and behaviors. The endangered proboscis monkey, found in Borneo's mangrove forests, is easily recognizable by its large, pendulous nose and swimming abilities.
Nusa Penida
The stunning island Nusa Penida, located southeast of Bali, Indonesia, is part of the Nusa Islands, which also include Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan. Nusa Penida is known for its rugged coastline, pristine beaches, and breathtaking natural landscapes, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers alike. JAPAN

Japan, an island nation in East Asia, is famous for its rich culture, technological prowess, and stunning landscapes. From the vibrant cities like Tokyo to the historic temples of Kyoto, Japan seamlessly blends tradition with innovation. Renowned for its cuisine, efficient transportation, and warm hospitality, Japan offers visitors an unforgettable experience of contrasts and harmony.
Geishas
The word Geisha isoften translated as "Woman of the arts," are iconic figures in Japanese culture. Dressed in elegant kimonos and adorned with distinctive white makeup, Geishas captivate audiences with traditional performances.
They entertain guests through music, dance, and sometimes engaging in drinking games. Before achieving the status of a Geisha, individuals undergo rigorous training as Maiko, mastering various arts and etiquette.
Sushi
A dish that represents the artistry and precision of Japanese cuisine is Sushi, it stands as one of Japan's most renowned culinary delights. This dish features vinegared rice combined with a variety of ingredients, including seafood like squid, eel, salmon, and tuna, both raw and cooked, as well as vegetables. Traditionally made with medium-grain white rice, Sushi is served alongside pickled ginger, wasabi, and soy sauce, creating a harmonious blend of flavors.

RUSSIA

With a population of over 12 million people, Moscow is the capital city of Russia and one of the most popular city in Europe. Known known both for the city's history and modernity, many tourists are interested to visit. The streets are lined with restaurants, shops, theaters and museums. As well the iconic landmarks as Red Square and Kremlin.
Matryoshka Dolls
Russia has a rich tradition of dolls that hold cultural, historical, and artistic significance. From the iconic Matryoshka nesting dolls to the lesser-known traditional rag dolls, these handcrafted objects offer insights into Russian culture, craftsmanship, and storytelling.
Russian dolls, from the classic Matryoshka to traditional rag dolls and contemporary art pieces, represent a rich tradition of craftsmanship and cultural symbolism. They continue to be a beloved part of Russian culture, with their intricate designs, meaningful themes, and enduring appeal. Whether as cherished toys, decorative items, or collectibles, Russian dolls reflect the creativity and heritage of Russian artisans and craftspeople.
Tourism
Russia's tourism industry boasts a diverse range of attractions. Moscow, the capital, is a vibrant metropolis known for its iconic landmarks such as the Kremlin.
Red Square, and St. Basil's Cathedral. The city's cultural scene includes the renowned Bolshoi Theatre, world-class museums like the Tretyakov Gallery, and a bustling nightlife. Moscow, the capital, is a vibrant metropolis known for its iconic landmarks such as the Kremlin, Red Square, and St. Basil's Cathedral. The city's cultural scene includes the renowned Bolshoi Theatre, world-class museums like the Tretyakov Gallery, and a bustling nightlife. Cultural festivals and events also play a significant role in Russian tourism. The White Nights Festival in St. Petersburg, with its dazzling performances and concerts, attracts visitors from around the world.

THAILAND

The capital city, Bangkok, is a dynamic metropolis filled with towering skyscrapers, ornate temples, and bustling markets. The Grand Palace, home to the revered Emerald Buddha, is a must-see landmark, reflecting the country's intricate craftsmanship and spiritual devotion. Meanwhile, the city's street food scene is legendary, offering an array of delicious dishes like pad thai, som tum, and mango sticky rice.
Buddhism
In Thailand, Buddhism plays a central role in the country's culture and daily life. The majority of Thais are Theravada Buddhists, and this religious tradition has a significant impact on society. Monks, known as "bhikkhus," are highly respected figures in Thailand, often seen walking barefoot through streets during their morning alms rounds, collecting offerings from the faithful. These alms, typically in the form of food or other necessities, reflect the Buddhist practice of generosity, or "dāna."

AUSTRALIA

Australia, known for its breathtaking nature and remarkable wildlife most of us would only dream to witness firsthand. This country has been a popular destination for curious adventures and nature enthusiasts for decades with tourism steadily increasing. Whether you’re visiting to seek out the incredible landscapes or just to experience whatever Australia has to offer, there are some famous natural landmarks that you just can’t miss. 
Natural landmarks
How about snorkeling through the coral reefs of the Great Barrier Reef, driving through the remote and vast inland areas of the Outback characterized by red desert, grassland and rocky ranges and let’s not forget about the iconic Kangaroo Island – the home of over 2000 species of plants and animals. If wildlife and nature is not your cup of tea, Australia still has numerous of intriguing attractions to explore within the cities. 
Sydney
The country’s largest city, offers some of the most iconic cultural landmarks like the Sydney Opera House, Sydney Harbor Bridge and plenty of coastal walks to name a few. Stroll around the city to encounter the buzzing atmosphere, remarkable architecture and vibrant history and experience what the Aussie charm is all about.
